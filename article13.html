Women powering through cup competition

By Eoghan Moloney

Ennis women made it two from two with a comprehensive win over UL Bohs on Saturday afternoon in Fr McNamara Park. A nip and tuck first half saw just a try separated the sides at half-time. However, the second half was dominated by the home side, who ran out deserving winners.

A Michelle Mescall run down the wing saw Lynda Daly gather and go over the line with less than two minutes played. From the kick-off, Ennis won possession and went forward with Robyn Conway, who bore down on the danger area. A scramble followed, with Bohs defending strongly but it was Chloe Neylon who got the last touch to crash down.

Play again moved forward, with Conway being fouled in possession and Finia O’Brien converting the resulting penalty. This time, Jane Clearywent on a marauding run forward, where Emma Mulrooney gathered and went between the posts. UL Bohs had their purple patch over the next 10 minutes, with three tries in quick succession. Bryant landed her next penalty kick in the 22 and in the scramble, it was tipped out over the line, which Bryant converted to level the sides.

Clare responded well, as Emily Cahill won possession and fed outside to her club mate Neylon, who edged the home side back in front with try in the corner. Cahill again won possession before picking out Lynda Daly, who added another point for the Banner side. Jane Lowry scored for the visitors and the last few minutes saw the sides exchange converted penalities, O’Brien for the home side and Bryant for the visitors, to see the sides finsih with just a try separating them.
