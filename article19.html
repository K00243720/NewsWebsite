Too easy for Inagh-Kilnamona

By Derrick Lynch

Inagh-Kilnamona got off to the better start in this senior camogie tie, with two early points from Aoife Keane. Clare Commane added another before Aine O’Loughlin got the opening score for the home side in the 11th minute.

In cold conditions, it was the visitors who dominated proceedings in the opening half, with Aoibhinn Ryan converting a free for the next score.

Commane, following good support work, grabbed the game’s first goal with Ryan following up with another point to leave the half-time score in favour of the visitors: Inagh-Kilnamona 1-5 Truagh-Clonlara 0-1.

Truagh-Clonlara tore into the second half with the impressive Eimear Kelly getting the first score of the half in the first minute followed by a free with just three minutes played, only for Aoife Keane to cancel this out when she grabbed the visitor’s second goal.

However, a fight back from Truagh-Clonlara followed with a Roisin Begley goal bookended by a brace of Kelly points to narrow the deficit to four points. Inagh-Kilnamona responded well, Aoibhinn Ryan converting a free, and the visitor’s third goal then came from the boot of Teresa O’Keeffe to effectively put the game out of reach of Truagh-Clonlara.

There was one last attack for Truagh-Clonlara with Kelly pointing but it was the reigning League champions who came away with the victory.

Michelle Mescall, Clare Hehir and Aoife Keane impressed for Inagh-Kilnamona whilst Eimear Kelly was very impressive for Truagh-Clonlara.

Inagh Kilnamona: Rebecca Keating; Jane Fitzgerald, Michelle Mescall, Michaela Roughan; Sinead Power, Clare Hehir, Orlaith Cotter; Andrea O’Keeffe, Hannah Shannon; Aoife Keane, Fiona Lafferty, Teresa O’Keeffe; Aoibhinn Ryan, Clare Commane, Emer Cotter. Subs: Tara Rynne for Emer Cotter, Cathy Cullinan for Hannah Shannon
